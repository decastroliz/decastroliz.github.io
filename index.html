Sisig is Pampangas 

all time favorite dish

1

Ingredients

Pigs ears
pigs face
Pigs brain (less than 100 g.)
Medium onion, minced
Siling labuyo, minced
Siling pang-sigang (long green Chili), minced and de-seeded
Sisig Sauce:
Squeezed Calamansi (2-4 pcs)
Kikoman soy sauce
Salt and Pepper to taste 

2

INSTRUCTIONS:

Prepare and clean pork and make sure there are no traces of hair in the skin.
Start boiling pork face and ears in pressure cooker for 7 minutes (start timer after it whistles). Turn of heat and drain water.
Roast the pork until the skin is brown and crispy.
Chop the pork meat into very small pieces. Set aside.
For the Pigs brain: Place it in plastic and seal and drop it in a pot of boiling water. Cook for less than 5 minutes. Do not over cook. Scoop out and set aside. 

3

Assemble:

Mix all the ingredients for the sisig sauce.
Mix together the sisig sauce, chopped pork, chopped onions, chopped siling labuyo, and siling haba.
Add the cooked pigs brain, and mix well.
Season with additional salt and ground pepper to taste.
Serve it in sizzling plate topped with egg.

SHAWRICE

Shawrice is a perfect combo meal for students who love to eat shawarma with rice. It perfectly fit for breakfast, lunch & merienda. In this branch of shawrice it will not test your patience because their servings is fast and nice. But when were run out of time, Shawrice is one of our mabilisan lang. Then the price of their Shawrice is such a student price especially their combo meals, Shawrice with Lemonade in a glass and their creamy garlic mayonnaise sauce.

INGREDIENTS:

Shawarma rice
Beef Tapa
Oil for cooking tapa
Cucumber salad
Garlic yogurt sauce 

            INSTRUCTIONS: 

For Shawarma Rice:
2 cups long grain rice (Jasmine or basmati)
2 cups water
1/4 cup margarine
1/2 tsp kasubha (crocus in english or dried safflower)
For Beef Tapa:
500 g beef sirloin, tapa cut
1/4 cup soy sauce
2 tbsp brown sugar
1 tsp garlic powder
1/2 tsp black pepper
Birds eye chili, optional

In a bowl combine together all ingredients for garlic yogurt sauce. Keep mixed sauce in the chiller until ready to use.
Rinse rice, put in a rice cooker together with water, margarine and kasubha. Cook rice as we would normally do. Once cooked, fluff rice to distribute color. Set aside and keep warm.
For the beef:
In a bowl, marinate beef in soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder and black pepper for 20 minutes or overnight.
Pan fry marinated tapa in oil until browned. Chop into pieces.
To assemble, put rice on plate, cucumber salad on the side.
Top minced tapa over rice.
Drizzle garlic yogurt sauce all over the beef & rice.
Just put a drop of hot sauce over your garlic sauce. And your diner can choose from regular sauce or hot sauce.
